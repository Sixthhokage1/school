Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun. Because Mercury is so close to the sun, there is no atmosphere as the Sun's solar winds blow it off. Therefore the surface of Mercury is covered with craters from meteorites that do not burn up on their way to the surface and the lack of atmosphere prevents the craters from being eroded away.
